How Dashboard Malfunctions in Electric Cars Can Signal Bigger Problems?

Imagine driving your electric car, enjoying the quiet ride, when suddenly, your dashboard flickers, shows incorrect information, or even goes completely dark. It might seem like a minor inconvenience at first. However, dashboard malfunctions in electric cars can be much more than a simple glitch; they could be warning signs of serious underlying issues. From battery health concerns to software malfunctions, these problems should never be ignored.

In this blog, we’ll explore why dashboard malfunctions happen, what they might indicate, and how they can be linked to larger problems in your electric vehicle. If you own an electric car or are considering getting one, understanding these signs can help you maintain your vehicle’s efficiency and safety.

What Is an Electric Car Dashboard and Why Is It Important?

The dashboard in an electric car is much more than just a screen displaying your speed. It serves as the communication hub between the car and the driver, providing crucial real-time data such as:

  • Battery charge level
  • Speed and mileage
  • Regenerative braking information
  • Software updates and alerts
  • Tire pressure monitoring
  • Warning signals for potential issues

If the dashboard stops working correctly, you may miss important alerts that can affect your safety and the overall performance of the vehicle.

Common Dashboard Malfunctions in Electric Cars

There are several ways an electric vehicle (EV) dashboard might malfunction. Here are some of the most common problems:

Flickering or Blank Screen

A dashboard screen that flickers or turns off unexpectedly can indicate software bugs, faulty wiring, or an issue with the car’s internal power distribution.

Incorrect or Missing Information

If your dashboard shows the wrong battery percentage, incorrect speed, or missing notifications, it can lead to miscalculations in charging and range estimation.

Unresponsive Touchscreen or Freezing

Many electric cars use touchscreens to control major functions, including climate settings and navigation. If the screen becomes unresponsive, it can make driving frustrating and unsafe.

Warning Lights Staying On or Off Incorrectly

A warning light that stays on even when there’s no issue or one that doesn’t turn on when needed can mean a deeper electronic problem, possibly related to sensors or the vehicle’s internal system.

What Causes Dashboard Malfunctions in Electric Cars?

Software Issues and Glitches

Electric cars rely heavily on software. Sometimes, a bug in the system or a failed update can cause dashboard malfunctions. Resetting the system or updating the software can sometimes resolve the issue.

Battery and Power System Problems

Since electric cars depend on a high-voltage battery system, any disruption in power flow can impact the dashboard. A weak or faulty 12V battery (which supports the car’s electronics) is a common cause of dashboard failures.

Faulty Wiring or Connections

Loose or damaged wiring can lead to erratic behavior in the dashboard, affecting the display, warning lights, and touchscreen responsiveness.

Sensor Failures

Electric cars have multiple sensors that monitor everything from battery temperature to regenerative braking. If one of these sensors malfunctions, it can send incorrect data to the dashboard or fail to trigger necessary warnings.

Why You Shouldn’t Ignore Dashboard Malfunctions?

Early Warning Signs of Battery Issues

A dashboard malfunction could be a sign of a deeper battery issue, such as overheating, charging irregularities, or even potential battery failure. Catching these problems early can prevent expensive repairs.

Potential Safety Risks

Incorrect dashboard readings can lead to dangerous situations, such as running out of charge unexpectedly or missing a crucial safety warning about tire pressure or brake issues.

Increased Repair Costs Over Time

If left unchecked, minor dashboard malfunctions can lead to more significant electrical failures, resulting in higher repair costs. Addressing these issues early can save money and prevent major breakdowns.

How to Fix and Prevent Dashboard Malfunctions in Your Electric Car?

Restart the System

Sometimes, a simple system reboot can fix minor software glitches. Try turning your vehicle off and on again to see if the issue resolves itself.

Check for Software Updates

Regularly updating your electric car’s software can prevent dashboard errors and ensure your vehicle operates smoothly. Manufacturers often release updates to fix known bugs and improve functionality.

Inspect the 12V Battery

Many dashboard malfunctions are linked to the 12V battery. If your car’s display is acting up, checking the 12V battery’s health can help identify whether it needs replacement.

Look for Loose or Damaged Wiring

A professional inspection of the vehicle’s wiring system can help diagnose any connectivity issues causing dashboard malfunctions.

Visit a Certified Electric Vehicle Technician

If dashboard issues persist, it’s best to consult a professional technician specializing in EVs. They have the right tools and expertise to diagnose and repair any underlying electrical problems.

Conclusion

A malfunctioning dashboard in an electric car is not just an annoyance; it can be an early warning sign of bigger problems. From battery health to software stability, these issues should never be ignored. If you experience dashboard failures, it’s essential to address them promptly to maintain safety and avoid costly repairs. For professional help with diagnosing and fixing electric vehicle issues, EVS Electric Vehicle Services provides expert solutions to keep your car running smoothly. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to dashboard malfunctions can ensure your electric car stays reliable and safe for the road ahead.

FAQ

Why does my electric car’s dashboard go blank while driving?

A blank dashboard can be caused by a software glitch, faulty wiring, or a weak 12V battery. Restarting the system or checking for updates may help.

Can a dashboard malfunction drain my electric car’s battery?

Yes, a faulty 12V battery or electrical malfunction can cause excessive power consumption, potentially leading to dashboard malfunctions, reduced efficiency, and unexpected battery drainage in your electric vehicle.

What should I do if my EV’s dashboard freezes?

Reset the car’s infotainment system or reboot the vehicle to resolve the issue. If the problem continues, seek a professional diagnostic check to identify and fix the malfunction.

Are dashboard problems common in electric cars?

Dashboard malfunctions are uncommon but can happen, particularly in cars with outdated software or electrical problems. Regular updates and maintenance help prevent these issues and ensure smooth operation.

Can dashboard malfunctions affect other car functions?

Yes, because many EV functions rely on the dashboard system, malfunctions can affect climate control, navigation, and other key settings, potentially disrupting the vehicle’s overall performance and user experience.

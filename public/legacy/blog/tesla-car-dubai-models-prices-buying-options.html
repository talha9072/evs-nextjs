Ultimate Guide to Tesla Cars in Dubai: Models, Prices, and Buying Options in 2025

Have you ever imagined gliding silently through Dubai’s streets in a sleek, futuristic Tesla?

With zero engine noise, high speed, and advanced self-driving features, Tesla is changing how we think about driving. If you’re planning to buy an electric car in 2025, Tesla should definitely be on your list.

In this guide, we’ll break down Tesla latest car models, help you compare them, share their prices in Dubai, and explain how you can buy one today. Whether you’re new to electric vehicles or a longtime fan of Elon Musk’s company, this blog gives you everything you need in one place in clear, simple words.

Tesla Car Models List in 2025

In 2025, Tesla will offer a full range of cars in Dubai to fit every lifestyle. All Tesla models available this year include the Tesla Model 3, Tesla Model Y, Tesla Model S, Tesla Model X, Tesla Cybertruck, and Tesla Roadster.

Each one is fully electric, offers smart features, and comes with Tesla’s famous Autopilot system. Let’s now explore each model in more detail.

Tesla Model 3

Tesla Model 3 remains the go-to model for most drivers in Dubai. It’s affordable, fast, and packed with smart features. In 2025, the Model 3 offers up to 600 kilometers on a full charge. The Performance model can go from 0 to 100 km/h in under 4 seconds, while the Standard version is still quick and efficient. The interior is simple but elegant, with a big central touchscreen, smooth driving controls, and Tesla’s latest self-driving software.

Tesla Model Y

Tesla Model Y is perfect for families. It’s a compact SUV with space for up to seven passengers. The 2025 version has a range of around 530 kilometers and comes with plenty of legroom, safety features, and a sleek design. It feels like driving a larger Model 3, but with more space and comfort.

Tesla Model S

Tesla Model S is the luxury sedan in Tesla’s lineup. It’s fast, stylish, and built for long-distance comfort. The 2025 Model S can drive over 650 kilometers on a full battery. If you choose the Plaid version, you get insane speed from 0 to 100 km/h in less than 2 seconds. Inside, you get a unique yoke-style steering wheel, wireless gaming features, and seats that feel like they belong in a private jet.

Tesla Model X

Tesla Model X is the SUV for those who want everything, such as performance, space, and tech. With falcon-wing doors, seating for seven, and a range of about 580 kilometers, the 2025 Model X turns heads wherever it goes. It has Tesla’s advanced driver-assist systems and can launch from 0 to 100 km/h in under 3 seconds in the Plaid variant.

Tesla Cybertruck

Tesla Cybertruck is the boldest Tesla yet. With a stainless-steel body and futuristic design, this truck is built for both work and style. It can tow heavy loads (over 6,000 kg) and offers a driving range of more than 800 kilometers in the tri-motor version. The inside is spacious and simple, with a massive central screen and rugged materials.

Tesla Roadster

Tesla Roadster is expected to launch in Dubai later in 2025. This car is built for pure speed and fun. It promises a range of up to 1,000 kilometers and can go from 0 to 100 km/h in under 2 seconds. It’s low to the ground, beautiful, and made for car lovers who want the fastest EV ever made.

Each of these cars gives you something different, from compact and affordable to high-end and high-speed. When it comes to Tesla car models comparison, you just have to pick what suits your needs and budget best.

Tesla Car Models and Prices in Dubai (2025)

Here’s a quick look at Tesla car all models in Dubai this year. Prices may vary based on features, trim level, and import fees:

In 2025, Tesla Model 3 starts from around AED 180,000, making it one of the most affordable Tesla options in Dubai.

Tesla Model Y, with its added space and SUV design, begins at approximately AED 210,000.

For those seeking luxury and high performance, the Tesla Model S is priced at AED 400,000, while the larger Tesla Model X starts at AED 420,000.

The highly anticipated Tesla Cybertruck is expected to launch with a starting price of around AED 300,000, depending on the version and configuration.

Lastly, the elite Tesla Roadster, known for its speed and sleek design, is expected to cost above AED 800,000.

These prices reflect base models. Choosing extra features like Full Self-Driving, premium interiors, or performance upgrades will raise the cost.

Buying a Tesla in Dubai: Your Options

If you’re ready to buy a Tesla in Dubai in 2025, you have several options to get your hands on different Tesla models.

  1. Tesla Website
    You can order your Tesla directly from the official Tesla UAE website. It’s easy to choose your model, color, wheels, and add features like enhanced Autopilot or Full Self-Driving. You’ll see the total price and estimated delivery time.
  2. Tesla Showroom
    Visit Tesla’s showroom in Dubai to see the cars in person. The staff can help you with test drives, trade-ins, and choosing the right model. It’s a good idea if you want to see and feel the car before making a decision.
  3. Used Tesla Market
    There’s also a growing used Tesla market in Dubai. Platforms like Dubizzle and CarSwitch list pre-owned Tesla cars at lower prices, and you can get Tesla models and prices on the website. Just make sure to check battery condition, warranty, and software version.
  4. Import from the US or Europe
    Some buyers choose to import their Tesla from the US or Europe. While you may get different pricing or features, it’s important to consider shipping fees, customs duties, and compatibility with local charging infrastructure.

Charging and Owning a Tesla in Dubai

Worried about charging your Tesla? Don’t be.

Dubai has an ever-expanding network of charging stations. Tesla’s own Superchargers are now available at malls and hotels. DEWA also supports EV Green Chargers across the city.

Charging at home is also simple. Tesla provides wall connectors you can install in your garage. A full overnight charge usually gives you enough battery for the whole week if you drive within the city.

Tesla’s mobile app lets you track charging, plan routes, schedule service, and even pre-cool your car before you get in, all from your phone.

Final Thoughts

Tesla is not just a car brand; it’s a lifestyle. Whether you’re looking for speed, space, savings, or sustainability, Tesla has something for everyone in Dubai in 2025. With six exciting models, smart features, and better charging than ever, there’s never been a better time to go electric, and we hope the Tesla models explained are suitable for you.

From Tesla car models and prices to how they compare, we hope this guide has helped you understand your options. Now, the only question is, which Tesla will you drive next?

FAQ

Which Tesla is best for families in Dubai?

The Tesla Model Y is a great pick for families. It has space for up to seven people, offers great safety features, and a smooth drive. It’s perfect for school runs, shopping, or weekend road trips around the UAE.

How much does a Tesla cost in Dubai in 2025?

Prices start around AED 180,000 for the Model 3 and go above AED 800,000 for the Roadster. EVS UAE lists updated prices for all Tesla cars in one place so you can compare models and make smart decisions.

Can I charge my Tesla at home in Dubai?

Yes, you can install a Tesla wall connector in your garage or parking space. It charges the car overnight so you’re ready to go in the morning. EVS UAE also offers advice on charging setups and local installation support.

Is Tesla good for long drives in the UAE?

Definitely. Most Tesla models offer 500–800 kilometers per charge. You can easily drive from Dubai to Abu Dhabi or even further without range anxiety. Superchargers along the way make it easy to top up your battery when needed.

What is the difference between Tesla Model 3 and Model Y?

Model 3 is a sleek sedan, while Model Y is a compact SUV with more space and higher seating. Both share many features, but the Model Y is better if you want extra room or have a bigger family.

Does Dubai have enough charging stations for Tesla?

Yes, there are many charging stations across the city, including Tesla Superchargers and DEWA Green Chargers. Most malls, hotels, and public parking spots offer EV charging, making it easy to keep your Tesla powered up every day.

What’s the fastest Tesla I can get in Dubai?

The Tesla Roadster is the fastest, going from 0 to 100 km/h in under 2 seconds. It’s expected to launch in Dubai later in 2025. It’s made for performance lovers who want speed and cutting-edge electric tech.
